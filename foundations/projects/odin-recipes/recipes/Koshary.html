Koshary

By Dina Said - Own work, CC BY-SA 4.0, Link

Description

Koshary is the quintessential Egyptian comfort food and the country's most beloved street food. It is a hearty, texturally diverse dish that beautifully combines several carbohydrates—rice, macaroni, and lentils—topped with chickpeas, crispy fried onions, and a punchy, zesty tomato-vinegar sauce.

This dish is naturally vegan and a staple in Coptic households during fasting periods. It’s a masterclass in layering flavors, where the savory lentils meet the sharpness of the garlic-vinegar sauce to create a deeply satisfying, multi-layered experience.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup brown lentils
  • 1 cup Egyptian short-grain rice
  • 1 cup elbow macaroni
  • 1 cup chickpeas (cooked)
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups tomato sauce
  • 2 tablespoons white vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Onions: Heat oil in a pan and fry the thinly sliced onions until they are deeply golden and crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels. Reserve the flavorful oil.
  2. Cook the Base: Boil the lentils until tender but firm. In a separate pot, cook the macaroni. In a third pot, cook the rice using some of the reserved onion oil for flavor.
  3. Make the Sauce: In a saucepan, sauté the minced garlic in a bit of the onion oil. Add the tomato sauce, vinegar, cumin, salt, and pepper. Simmer until thickened.
  4. Assemble: In a serving bowl, create a base layer of rice, followed by a layer of lentils and macaroni.
  5. Top and Serve: Spoon the tomato sauce over the top, add the chickpeas, and generously garnish with the crispy fried onions. Serve hot.
