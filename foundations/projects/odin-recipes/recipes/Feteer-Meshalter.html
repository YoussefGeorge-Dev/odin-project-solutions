Feteer Meshaltet

By Mohamed Ouda - Own work, CC BY-SA 4.0, Link

Description

Feteer Meshaltet is a traditional Egyptian layered pastry, often referred to as "Egyptian pizza" or a savory-sweet pie. It consists of dozens of paper-thin layers of dough folded with ghee or vegetable oil, resulting in a bread that is incredibly flaky on the outside and soft and chewy on the inside.

A symbol of Egyptian hospitality, it is frequently served to guests. Whether enjoyed plain, with honey and cheese for breakfast, or as a hearty meal, Feteer Meshaltet is a true labor of love that showcases the artistry of Egyptian baking.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup ghee, softened vegetable oil, or margarine (for the "Siami" fasting version)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a large bowl, mix the flour, salt, and water. Knead thoroughly for at least 10 minutes until the dough is smooth, elastic, and does not stick to your hands.
  2. Rest: Divide the dough into small balls, coat them with a little oil, and let them rest for at least 30–60 minutes. This is crucial for successful stretching.
  3. Stretch and Layer: On a well-oiled surface, take one ball and stretch it until it is paper-thin. Brush it with ghee/oil, then fold it into a square. Repeat this process for multiple balls, layering the folded squares inside one another.
  4. Shape: Once all layers are stacked and folded, place the final large square into a round baking pan and gently flatten it to fill the pan.
  5. Bake: Bake in a preheated oven at 200°C for 20–25 minutes, or until the pastry is puffed, golden brown, and crispy. Serve warm.
