Umm Ali

By Miansari66 - Own work, CC0, Link

Description

Umm Ali is one of Egypt's most famous and beloved desserts, often described as an Egyptian bread pudding. This rich, creamy, and decadent treat consists of layers of puff pastry or dried bread soaked in sweetened hot milk, topped with a generous mix of nuts, raisins, and coconut, and baked until golden and bubbling.

It is the perfect comfort food—warm, incredibly fragrant, and deeply satisfying. Its name has legendary roots, and it remains a staple at celebrations, gatherings, and family dinners throughout Egypt.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups puff pastry, baked and broken into small pieces (or dried croissant pieces)
  • 4 cups whole milk (or a mix of milk and heavy cream)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup golden raisins
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds or pistachios
  • 2 tablespoons shredded coconut
  • 1 tablespoon butter or ghee

Steps

  1. Prepare the Base: Preheat your oven to 180°C. In a heat-proof baking dish, distribute the pieces of broken puff pastry evenly.
  2. Add Toppings: Sprinkle the raisins, sliced nuts, and shredded coconut over the pastry pieces in the dish.
  3. Sweeten the Milk: In a saucepan, heat the milk with the sugar, vanilla, and butter until the sugar is fully dissolved and the milk is hot (but not boiling).
  4. Combine: Pour the hot, sweetened milk over the pastry and nuts in the baking dish. Ensure all pieces are well-soaked.
  5. Bake: Place the dish in the oven and bake for 15–20 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the milk has been partially absorbed. Serve warm.
